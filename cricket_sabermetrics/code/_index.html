IPL 2019: Who needs what to win ?

A sabermetrics based approach to selecting the perfect T20 team.

Introduction

IPL 2018 featured teams comprising of newly selected players. The selection process at the auction has improved vastly over the 10 years of IPL and this resulted in IPL 2018 being arguably the most closely fought season. However over the course of the tournament, it was evident that not all the teams were well balanced ultimately costing them places in the playoffs. This brings us to the possibility of building that all elusive perfect T20 team. Can we quantify the ...

  • Team composition of champion T20 teams
  • Winning formula of champion T20 teams
  • X-factor wins
  • IPL 2019: Who is missing what ?
  • Scouting guide: Where to find the missing pieces ?

This article aims to answer the above questions in an attempt to discover the formula to win a T20 competition. But how much can we rely on past numbers alone ? Beauty of sports lies in its uncertainity. On any day, any given player can completely overcome all the odds of performing and win the game for his team. That is the reason we all watch sports and the resulting adrenaline rush. Nevertheless it will be interesting to see how far can past numbers tell us the present story and predict the future outcomes.

Team composition

Can we put together a team which will maximise the chances of winning ? If so what type of players should constitute the batting and bowling units ? How many and what type of allrounders should be included ? Let us the try to answer these questions by studying the batting and bowling metrics of the T20 league champions from across the world.

We begin by looking at the batting chart of IPL 2018. The horizontal axis of this chart shows the average of the batsman whereas the vertical axis shows the strike-rate of the batsman. Using this chart, we can categorize the batsmen into roughly four types.

  • Duds
  • Accumulators
  • Strikers
  • Ideals

We can perform a similar analysis for bowlers by looking at the bowling chart of IPL 2018. The horizontal axis of this chart shows the economy rate of the bowler whereas the vertical axis shows the strike-rate of the bowler. Using this chart, we can categorize the bowlers into roughly four types.

  • Duds
  • Containers
  • Strikers
  • Ideals

IPL 2018 batting chart
IPL 2018 bowling chart

Given a chance, all the teams would like to have their batting lineups filled with Ideals and no teams would want to have duds. But while it is likely any player could be an Ideal during his/her purple patch, it is very unlikely that the entire lineup ends up being Ideal during a season. This warrants us to have a team with combinations of Accumulators, Strikers and Ideals. But how many of these different players does a team need ? Too many Accumulators in the top order often results in sub 200 score even with good starts whereas a team filled with Strikers will invariably have scores either around the 200 mark or have batting collapses with scores around the 140 runs mark. Similarly for bowling, the question is how many of each kind of these bowlers does a team need in order to increase its chances of winning ?. So how many Accumulators and Strikers does a team really need ? A look at the batting and bowling metrics of few of the T20 league champions during the 2018 season provides an insight for this elusive winning combination.
